HOME ECONOMICS

Home Economics, commonly known as H.E, is an interdisciplinary field of study that encompasses practical skills and theoretical knowledge related to the management of household resources, including nutrition, textiles, housing, and consumer economics.

Taking H.E strand provides students with practical skills that can be applied in real-life situations, making them more employable and better equipped to manage their households. It also helps them develop a sense of responsibility, independence, and self-confidence, which are essential qualities for personal growth and development.

WHAT IS FBS?

Food and Beverage Services and Bartending. In Food and Beverage Services, students learn how to serve food properly, including table etiquette and the sequence of services. They also study the manners of a waiter and how to provide excellent customer service.

WHAT IS BARTENDING?

In Bartending, students learn about different types of alcohol, how to flip bottles, and the manners of a bartender. They also study the different types of restaurants and their roles in the food industry.

H.E strand covers a range of skills related to the food and beverage industry in its Food Beverage Services and Bartending components. In Food Beverage Services, students learn the:



  • practical aspects of serving food
  • table setting
  • food presentation, and customer service.
  • proper etiquette in table settings
  • sequence of services. Bartending, on the other hand, focuses on the study of:
  • different types of alcohol
  • bottle flipping techniques
  • bartender manners
